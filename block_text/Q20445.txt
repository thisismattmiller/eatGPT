Martin: The Ballets Russes and Ballets Suédois but they were not quite the same. In those cases you had strong impresarios—[Sergei] Diaghilev and Rolf de Maré—commissioning artists and composers and choreographers to make very finished works for a very elite audience. The artists were advanced avant-garde artists and some of the work was shocking to its audience, like [Vaslav] Nijinsky and Rite of Spring in 1913. But the goal was working together for very finished works for a ballet public. They were professional dance companies.
