While 20 helium-filled pillows floated eerily in a two-story room called “the Chapel,” the need for bringing artist and technologist together was stressed in speeches by Senator Jacob K. Javits, a traditionalist who sees merit in modern art; Ralph C. Gross, president of the Commerce and Industry Association; Edwin Langsam, film projection supervisor of the American Telephone and Telegraph Company; Herman D. Kenin, head of the A.F.L.-C.I.O.’s new Scientific Professional and Cultural Employees Council; and Dr. Warren Brodey, a psychiatrist, of Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
